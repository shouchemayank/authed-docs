## Ahsoka Tano

Ahsoka Tano is a Togruta female who was discovered by Jedi Master Plo Koon and brought to the Jedi Temple on Coruscant at a young age. She became the Padawan learner of Anakin Skywalker during the Clone Wars, where she earned the nickname **Snips** from her master.

### Early life and the Clone Wars

Ahsoka was assigned to Anakin Skywalker as his Padawan by Jedi Grand Master Yoda. Initially, Anakin was reluctant to take on an apprentice, but the two quickly formed a close bond. Throughout the Clone Wars, Ahsoka proved herself as a skilled and courageous fighter, participating in key battles such as the Battle of Christophsis, the rescue of Jabba the Hutt's son, and the Siege of Mandalore.

### Leaving the Jedi Order

After being falsely accused of bombing the Jedi Temple hangar, Ahsoka was expelled from the Jedi Order and put on trial. Although she was ultimately exonerated when the true perpetrator, Barriss Offee, was revealed, the experience shattered her trust in the Order. She made the difficult decision to walk away from the Jedi, becoming one of the first characters in Star Wars canon to leave on her own terms.

### Life after the Jedi

Following Order 66 and the fall of the Republic, Ahsoka went into hiding. She eventually resurfaced as the mysterious rebel operative known as **Fulcrum**, providing intelligence to various rebel cells during the early days of the Rebellion against the Galactic Empire. She wielded twin white lightsabers, symbolizing her independence from both the Jedi and the Sith.

### Key traits

- **Dual-wielding lightsaber combat** — Ahsoka is known for her reverse-grip Shien fighting style using two lightsabers
- **Strong moral compass** — She consistently makes choices based on what she believes is right, even when it conflicts with authority
- **Mentorship** — Over time, Ahsoka becomes a mentor figure herself, guiding others such as Sabine Wren and Ezra Bridger
- **Resilience** — From Padawan to independent Force wielder, Ahsoka's journey is defined by her ability to grow through adversity
