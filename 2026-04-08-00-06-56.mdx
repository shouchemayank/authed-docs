## Boba Fett

Boba Fett is one of the most iconic characters in the Star Wars galaxy. A Mandalorian bounty hunter, he is the unaltered clone of the famed bounty hunter Jango Fett, raised as his son on the ocean planet of Kamino.

### Origins

Boba Fett was created on Kamino as part of the clone army project, but unlike the millions of other clones produced for the Galactic Republic, he was unmodified — no accelerated aging, no behavioral conditioning. Jango Fett requested him as payment for serving as the clone template, raising Boba as his own child.

After Jango's death at the Battle of Geonosis at the hands of Jedi Master Mace Windu, young Boba was left to forge his own path in a dangerous galaxy.

### Career as a bounty hunter

Boba Fett built a reputation as one of the most feared and effective bounty hunters in the galaxy. Key highlights of his career include:

- **Working for Jabba the Hutt** — Fett was a frequent contractor for the Hutt crime lord, taking on high-value targets across the Outer Rim.
- **Capturing Han Solo** — Hired by Darth Vader to track down the Millennium Falcon, Fett successfully captured Han Solo and delivered him, frozen in carbonite, to Jabba the Hutt.
- **The Sarlacc Pit** — During the rescue of Han Solo at the Great Pit of Carkoon, Fett fell into the Sarlacc. Against all odds, he survived.

### Legacy

After escaping the Sarlacc, Boba Fett eventually claimed the throne of Jabba's former criminal empire on Tatooine. He transitioned from a lone bounty hunter to a leader seeking to rule with respect rather than fear.

<Note>
Boba Fett first appeared in the Star Wars Holiday Special in 1978 before making his big-screen debut in *The Empire Strikes Back* (1980).
</Note>
