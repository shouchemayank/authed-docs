## Din Djarin

Din Djarin, known to many as "the Mandalorian," is a foundational warrior raised in the ways of the Mandalorian creed. Orphaned during the Clone Wars, he was rescued and adopted by the Children of the Watch, a sect of Mandalorians who adhere to strict traditions — including never removing their helmet in front of another living being.

### Background

Din Djarin works as a bounty hunter across the outer rim of the galaxy, taking jobs from the Bounty Hunters' Guild. His life changes when he accepts a high-value commission from a mysterious Imperial remnant client to retrieve a child — later known as Grogu.

Rather than hand Grogu over, Din breaks his contract and goes on the run, becoming the child's protector and surrogate father.

### Key traits

- **Creed adherence:** Din follows the Way of the Mandalore, living by the code "This is the Way"
- **Combat proficiency:** Skilled with blasters, a vibroblade, and his full beskar armor
- **Ship:** Originally pilots the Razor Crest, a pre-Empire gunship, before later acquiring a modified Naboo N-1 starfighter
- **Relationships:** Forms deep bonds with Grogu, fellow Mandalorian Bo-Katan Kryze, and allies like Greef Karga and Cara Dune

### The Darksaber

Din comes into possession of the Darksaber after defeating Moff Gideon in combat. The Darksaber is a unique black-bladed lightsaber created by Tarre Vizsla, the first Mandalorian Jedi. Traditionally, whoever wields the Darksaber holds a claim to rule Mandalore.

<Note>
Din Djarin first appeared in *The Mandalorian* (2019), a live-action series set five years after *Return of the Jedi*.
