## Ahsoka Tano

Ahsoka Tano is a Togruta female who was discovered by Jedi Master Plo Koon as a young child and brought to the Jedi Temple on Coruscant for training. She became the Padawan learner of Anakin Skywalker during the Clone Wars, where she proved herself as a courageous and resourceful fighter.

### Early life

Ahsoka was born on the planet Shili and was identified as Force-sensitive at a young age. She was taken to the Jedi Order and eventually assigned as Padawan to Anakin Skywalker by Grand Master Yoda — a pairing designed to teach Anakin the value of letting go.

### The Clone Wars

During the Clone Wars, Ahsoka served as a commander in the Grand Army of the Republic. She fought in numerous battles across the galaxy, including:

- The Battle of Christophsis
- The rescue of Jabba the Hutt's son, Rotta
- The Siege of Mandalore

Her experiences during the war shaped her into a skilled warrior and leader, though they also exposed her to the corruption and moral ambiguity within the Republic and the Jedi Order.

### Leaving the Jedi Order

After being falsely accused of bombing the Jedi Temple hangar, Ahsoka was expelled from the Order and put on trial. Though she was ultimately exonerated when Barriss Offee was revealed as the true culprit, the experience shattered her trust in the Jedi Council. She chose to walk away from the Order, one of the most pivotal moments in her story.

### The Rebellion era

After the fall of the Republic and the rise of the Empire, Ahsoka operated under the codename **Fulcrum**, providing intelligence to various rebel cells. She played a key role in the early formation of the Rebel Alliance and later confronted her former master, now Darth Vader, in a dramatic duel on Malachor.

### Legacy

Ahsoka Tano is one of the most beloved characters in the Star Wars universe, first introduced in the animated film *Star Wars: The Clone Wars* (2008) and later appearing in *Star Wars Rebels*, *The Mandalorian*, and her own live-action series *Ahsoka*. She represents resilience, moral courage, and the idea that you can walk your own path outside of rigid institutions.
