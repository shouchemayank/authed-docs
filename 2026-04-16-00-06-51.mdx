## Boba Fett

Boba Fett is one of the most iconic characters in the Star Wars universe. A Mandalorian bounty hunter, he is the unaltered clone of the famed bounty hunter Jango Fett, raised as his son on the ocean planet of Kamino.

### Origins

Boba was created on Kamino as part of the deal between Jango Fett and the Sith Lord Count Dooku. Unlike the millions of clone troopers produced for the Republic's army, Boba was unmodified — no accelerated aging, no behavioral conditioning. Jango raised him as a son, teaching him combat skills and the ways of bounty hunting from a young age.

After witnessing his father's death at the hands of Jedi Master Mace Windu during the Battle of Geonosis, Boba swore vengeance and began forging his own path as a bounty hunter.

### Career as a bounty hunter

Boba Fett became one of the most feared bounty hunters in the galaxy. He was frequently employed by the Galactic Empire and the crime lord Jabba the Hutt. His most notable accomplishment was tracking Han Solo to Cloud City on behalf of Darth Vader, leading to Solo's capture and encasement in carbonite.

<Note>
Boba Fett wears customized Mandalorian armor originally belonging to his father, Jango Fett. The armor includes a jetpack, wrist-mounted weapons, and a distinctive T-shaped visor.
</Note>

### Key appearances

- **The Empire Strikes Back** — Hired by Darth Vader to track the Millennium Falcon
- **Return of the Jedi** — Present at Jabba's Palace and the Battle of the Great Pit of Carkoon
- **The Mandalorian** — Returns after surviving the Sarlacc Pit
- **The Book of Boba Fett** — Takes over Jabba the Hutt's criminal empire on Tatooine

### Legacy

Despite his relatively limited screen time in the original trilogy, Boba Fett became a fan-favorite character. His mysterious demeanor, distinctive armor, and reputation as the galaxy's deadliest bounty hunter cemented his place in Star Wars lore. His story was significantly expanded in both the prequel trilogy and later Disney+ series.
