## Boba Fett

Boba Fett is one of the most iconic characters in the Star Wars universe. A Mandalorian bounty hunter, he is the unaltered clone of the famed bounty hunter Jango Fett, raised as his son on the ocean world of Kamino.

### Origins

Boba was created on Kamino as part of the clone army project, but unlike the millions of other clones produced for the Galactic Republic, he was unmodified — no accelerated aging, no behavioral conditioning. Jango requested him as payment for serving as the genetic template, raising Boba as a son and training him in combat and survival from an early age.

### Rise to infamy

After witnessing his father's death at the hands of Jedi Master Mace Windu during the Battle of Geonosis, Boba swore vengeance and embarked on a path that would make him the most feared bounty hunter in the galaxy. Key moments in his career include:

- **Working for the Empire** — Darth Vader personally hired Boba Fett to track down the Millennium Falcon and Han Solo
- **Capturing Han Solo** — He delivered the carbonite-frozen Solo to Jabba the Hutt, collecting one of the most famous bounties in galactic history
- **Surviving the Sarlacc** — Though presumed dead after falling into the Great Pit of Carkoon, Boba escaped the Sarlacc pit and eventually reclaimed Mandalorian armor

### Legacy

Boba Fett went on to claim the throne of Tatooine's underworld, ruling from Jabba's former palace. His story arc — from orphaned clone to ruthless bounty hunter to crime lord seeking to lead with respect rather than fear — makes him one of the most compelling figures in the Star Wars saga.

<Note>
  Despite having only a few minutes of screen time in the original trilogy, Boba Fett became one of the franchise's most popular characters, eventually earning his own series, *The Book of Boba Fett*.
</Note>
