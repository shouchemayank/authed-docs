## Ahsoka Tano

Ahsoka Tano is a Togruta from the planet Shili who became one of the most significant figures in the Star Wars galaxy. Originally introduced as Anakin Skywalker's Padawan during the Clone Wars, she grew into a skilled warrior and leader in her own right.

### Early life

Ahsoka was discovered by Jedi Master Plo Koon at a young age and brought to the Jedi Temple on Coruscant for training. She was assigned as a Padawan to Anakin Skywalker by Yoda during the Battle of Christophsis, a pairing that initially surprised both master and apprentice.

### The Clone Wars

During the Clone Wars, Ahsoka fought alongside Anakin and the 501st Legion in countless battles across the galaxy. She proved herself as a capable commander and lightsaber duelist, wielding a distinctive reverse grip with her green and later white lightsabers.

Key events during this period include:

- **Battle of Ryloth** — Led a squadron of starfighters in a critical engagement
- **Rescue on Mortis** — Encountered the Force wielders known as the Father, Son, and Daughter
- **Siege of Mandalore** — Captured the former Sith Lord Maul in one of the final battles of the Clone Wars

### Leaving the Jedi Order

After being falsely accused of bombing the Jedi Temple hangar, Ahsoka was expelled from the Order and put on trial. Though she was ultimately exonerated when the true culprit was found, the experience shattered her trust in the Jedi Council. She made the difficult decision to walk away from the Order, a moment that deeply affected her former master Anakin.

### The Rebellion era

Operating under the codename **Fulcrum**, Ahsoka became a key intelligence operative for the early Rebel Alliance. She provided critical information to rebel cells across the galaxy and eventually confronted her former master — now Darth Vader — in a dramatic duel on the Sith world of Malachor.

### Legacy

Ahsoka Tano stands as a symbol of independence and moral clarity in the Star Wars universe. Neither fully Jedi nor Sith, she charted her own path through the Force, guided by her own sense of justice rather than the dogma of any single tradition.
