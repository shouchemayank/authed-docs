## Ahsoka Tano

Ahsoka Tano is a Togruta female who served as the Padawan learner to Anakin Skywalker during the Clone Wars. First introduced in the 2008 animated film *Star Wars: The Clone Wars*, she quickly became one of the most beloved characters in the franchise.

### Early life

Ahsoka was discovered by Jedi Master Plo Koon on her homeworld of Shili at the age of three. She was brought to the Jedi Temple on Coruscant, where she trained as a youngling before being assigned to Anakin Skywalker as his apprentice by Jedi Grand Master Yoda.

### The Clone Wars

During the Clone Wars, Ahsoka proved herself a capable and resourceful warrior. She fought alongside her master in numerous battles, including:

- The Battle of Christophsis
- The rescue of Rotta the Hutt
- The siege of Mandalore

Her combat skills with dual lightsabers — a standard green blade and a shorter yellow-green shoto — became her signature fighting style.

### Leaving the Jedi Order

After being falsely accused of bombing the Jedi Temple hangar, Ahsoka was expelled from the Order and put on trial. Though she was ultimately proven innocent (the true culprit was her fellow Padawan Barriss Offee), the experience shattered her trust in the Jedi Council. She made the difficult decision to walk away from the Order.

### The Rebellion era

Ahsoka resurfaced years later under the codename **Fulcrum**, serving as an intelligence operative for the growing Rebel Alliance. She played a key role in organizing rebel cells across the galaxy before eventually confronting her former master — now Darth Vader — in a dramatic duel on Malachor.

### Legacy

Ahsoka Tano represents growth, independence, and moral courage. Her journey from eager Padawan to wise, independent Force-wielder has resonated with fans across generations. She is a reminder that you don't need a title to make a difference in the galaxy.

<Note>
  Ahsoka has appeared across multiple Star Wars media, including *The Clone Wars*, *Rebels*, *The Mandalorian*, and her own live-action series *Ahsoka* on Disney+.
</Note>
