## Din Djarin — The Mandalorian

Din Djarin, commonly known as "The Mandalorian" or "Mando," is a Mandalorian bounty hunter who operates in the outer reaches of the galaxy during the era of the New Republic. Orphaned as a child during the Clone Wars, he was rescued and raised by the Mandalorian warrior culture, adopting their creed and way of life.

### Background

Din Djarin follows the strict code of the Mandalorian creed — most notably the rule of never removing his helmet in front of others. His life takes a dramatic turn when he is hired to retrieve a bounty that turns out to be a Force-sensitive child of Yoda's species, whom he nicknames "Grogu."

Rather than hand the child over to remnants of the Galactic Empire, Djarin breaks his contract and becomes Grogu's protector. This decision sets him on a path that challenges his beliefs, forges unexpected alliances, and ultimately reshapes his identity as both a warrior and a father figure.

### Why he matters

- **Embodies the lone gunslinger archetype** — Djarin's story draws heavily from westerns and samurai cinema, grounding Star Wars in its genre roots.
- **Explores found family** — His bond with Grogu is the emotional core of the series and resonates across all audiences.
- **Expands Mandalorian lore** — Through Djarin, audiences learn about different Mandalorian factions, the Darksaber, and the planet Mandalore itself.
